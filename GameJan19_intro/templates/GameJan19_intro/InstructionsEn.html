First, all participants will be randomly divided into groups of four. This will be done anonymously, so that you will not know who else is in your group and your group will not know your identity.

Participants in a group will have different roles: Player 1A, Player 1B, Player 2C, and Player 2D. You will be assigned randomly to a role, and it will be displayed at the beginning of each round of the game.

There will be {{ n_rounds }} rounds in the game. Groups will be reshuffled after each round. Your role in a group might also change. However, if you were Player 1A or Player 1B in the first round, you will never be Player 2C or Player 2D in any subsequent round. Similarly, if you were Player 2C or Player 2D in the first round, you will never be Player 1A or Player 1B in any subsequent round.

Round of the game

  1. All participants with roles Player 1A and Player 1B are endowed with {{ endowment }} tokens. Each of them has to choose how much out of {{ endowment }} tokens to propose. This information remains private during the whole game. Denote by OfferA and OfferB Player 1A and Player 1B choices: both OfferA and OfferB are integers between 0 and {{ endowment }}.
  2. In each group, computer randomly chooses C or D. All four players in a group observe this choice. Denote by ComputerChoice the computer's choice: ComputerChoice is either C or D.
  3. If ComputerChoice=C, then Player 2C chooses A or B. If ComputerChoice=D, then Player 2D chooses A or B. All four players in a group observe this choice. Denote by PlayerChoice the above choice Player 2C or 2D made: PlayerChoice is either A or B.
  4. All participants with roles Player 2C and Player 2D has to decide on the minimum offer each of them would accept. This information remains private during the whole game. Denote by MaoC and MaoD Player 2C and Player 2D choices: both MaoC and MaoD are nonnegative integers.
  5. Payoff from a round is defined as follows. Based on ComputerChoice and PlayerChoice players are paired within a group.
    1. If ComputerChoice=C and PlayerChoice=A, then Player 1A is paired with Player 2C, while Player 1B is paired with Player 2D.
    2. If ComputerChoice=C and PlayerChoice=B, then Player 1B is paired with Player 2C, while Player 1A is paired with Player 2D.
    3. If ComputerChoice=D and PlayerChoice=A, then Player 1A is paired with Player 2D, while Player 1B is paired with Player 2C.
    4. If ComputerChoice=D and PlayerChoice=B, then Player 1B is paired with Player 2D, while Player 1A is paired with Player 2C.
    In each pair one participant has the role of Player 1 and the other participant has the role of Player 2. If Offer is greater or equal to Mao, then Player 1 gets {{ endowment }} - Offer tokens, while Player 2 gets Offer tokens. Otherwise, that is, if Offer is less than Mao, then both Player 1 and Player 2 get {{ payoff_if_rejected }} tokens.

Once all {{ n_rounds }} rounds are over, you will know your payoff in each round. Exactly one round will be randomly chosen for the actual payment.

After reading the instructions, each of you receive a small quiz that checks your understanding of these instructions. Your payment does not depend how well you perform on this quiz. However, if you answer a question incorrectly, you will have to answer the same question again.

After completing the game, you will be asked to answer a few more questions. You will be paid for some of them as well.
