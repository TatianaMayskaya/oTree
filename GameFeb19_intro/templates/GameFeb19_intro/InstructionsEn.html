Welcome to the experimental session! There will be {{ n_rounds }} rounds in the game, all conducted according to the same rules. In each round of the game, all participants in this session will be randomly divided into groups of four. This will be done anonymously, so that you will not know who else is in your group, and other group members will not know your identity either.

Participants in every group will have four different roles, denoted as: Player 1A, Player 1B, Player 2C, and Player 2D. In what follows, we refer to roles of Player 1A and Player 1B as to Player 1 roles, and to roles Player 2C and Player 2D as to Player 2 roles. You will be assigned randomly to one of these role, which will be displayed to you at the beginning of each round of the game.

Groups will be reshuffled after each round. That is, in each round you will be playing with different partners, and your role in a group might also change. However, if you were Player 1A or Player 1B in the first round, you will remain in the role of Player 1 (A or B), and will never be Player 2C or Player 2D in any subsequent round. Similarly, if you were Player 2C or Player 2D in the first round, you will remain in the role of Player 2 (C or D), and will never be Player 1A or Player 1B in any subsequent round.

We now describe to you the rules of the game, which are the same in every round.

Rules of the game.

Every round shall consist of four stages.

  1. At the first stage of every round, participants in roles of Player 1 (A and B) are endowed with {{ endowment }} tokens. Each of them has to choose how much out of {{ endowment }} tokens he or she wants to pass to one of the Players 2 (C or D, to be determined later) in their group (in nonnegative integer numbers, but no larger than {{ endowment }}). The rest each Player 1 keeps for him- or herself. These decisions of Players 1 will remain private during the whole game. Participants in roles of Player 2 (C and D) have to wait for these decisions.

  2. At the second stage of every round, the computer chooses at random one of the participants with Player 2 role (either Player 2C or Player 2D) who will be given the right to choose Player 1 (Player 1A or Player 1B) to be matched with. The other Player 2 will be automatically matched as partner with the other Player 1.

    If the computer has chosen Player 2C to make the decision, then Player 2C chooses either Player 1A or Player 1B to be matched with. Player 2D is then automatically matched with the other Player 1. That is, if Player 2C chooses to be matched with Player 1A, Player 2D shall be automatically matched with Player 1B. If Player 2C chooses to be matched with Player 1B, Player 2D shall be automatically matched with Player 1A.

    If the computer has chosen Player 2D to make the decision, then Player 2D chooses Player 1A or Player 1B to be matched with. Player 2C is then automatically matched with the other Player 1. That is, if Player 2D chooses to be matched with Player 1A, Player 2C shall be automatically matched with Player 1B. If Player 2D chooses to be matched with Player 1B, Player 2C shall be automatically matched with Player 1A.

    None of the Players 2 will know the decision of either Player 1A or Player 1B at this stage.

    Based on the procedure described above, at the end of this stage four players in each group shall be split in two pairs, and shall commonly observe this matching.

  3. At the third stage, all participants in roles of Player 2 (C and D) have to decide on the minimum offer from their partner Player 1 that each of them is willing to accept (in nonnegative integer numbers, but no larger than {{ endowment_plus_1 }}).

    As before, none of the Players 2 will know the decision of either Player 1A or Player 1B at this stage.

  4. Payoff from each round shall be determined separately for each matched pair as follows. Denote by Offer the amount passed by the Player 1. Denote by MinOffer the minimum acceptable offer chosen by the Player 2.

    If Offer of the Player 1 in the matched pair is greater or equal to MinOffer of the Player 2 in that pair, then the decision of the Player 1 is realised: the Player 2 in this pair gets Offer tokens, and the Player 1 gets {{ endowment }} - Offer tokens.

    If Offer of the Player 1 in the matched pair is less than MinOffer of the Player 2 in that pair, then the decision of the Player 1 is not realised, and both players in that pair get {{ payoff_if_rejected }} tokens.

After the whole experiment is over, all players shall be informed in private about the amount of tokens they have earned in each round. Exactly one round of the game will be randomly chosen for the actual payment. Each participant shall be paid in private the amount equivalent to their earning in that round according to the announced exchange rate of tokens to fiat money.

After reading the instructions, each of you receive a small quiz that checks your understanding of these instructions. Your payment does not depend on your performance in this quiz. However, if you answer a question incorrectly, you will have to answer the same question again.

After completing the game, you will be asked to answer a few more questions.

On the top of what you will earn during the experiment, you will get {{ show_up_text }} for participation.
